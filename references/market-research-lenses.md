# Market Research Lenses

Use when the user asks about a market, category, industry, customer segment, competitor set, TAM/SAM/SOM, demand, pricing, adoption, or go-to-market reality.

## Core Questions

- What exactly is the market boundary?
- Who is the buyer, user, payer, and budget owner?
- What segments behave differently?
- What is growing, shrinking, consolidating, or commoditizing?
- What substitutes or "do nothing" options compete with the category?
- What evidence is based on actual spend, usage, or procurement rather than vendor narrative?

## Evidence To Prefer

- Government statistics, industry associations, regulator data, trade publications
- Company filings, investor decks, annual reports, pricing pages, customer case studies
- Survey methodology pages, sample definitions, analyst report footnotes
- Search demand, app/package usage, hiring, procurement, reviews, communities, and channel checks

## Market Sizing Discipline

Separate:

- TAM: total theoretical market
- SAM: serviceable market under current geography/product/channel
- SOM: realistic obtainable market under near-term distribution constraints

For each estimate, record:

- unit, geography, year, currency, denominator, and source
- whether the estimate is top-down, bottom-up, or triangulated
- what assumption would most change the answer

## Output Checks

- Do not treat a large TAM as proof of a good opportunity.
- Separate market attractiveness from company/product attractiveness.
- Preserve disagreements between sources instead of averaging them away.
- State what is known, estimated, inferred, and still unknown.
