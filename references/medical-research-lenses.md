# Medical Research Lenses

Use when the topic involves healthcare, medicine, clinical evidence, drugs, devices, diagnostics, supplements, symptoms, treatments, safety, or medical regulation.

## Safety Boundary

- Do not diagnose.
- Do not prescribe treatment.
- Do not replace a clinician or emergency care.
- Make medical uncertainty explicit.
- Encourage professional medical advice for personal health decisions.

## Evidence Hierarchy

Prefer:

- clinical guidelines from recognized professional bodies
- public health agencies and regulators
- systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- randomized controlled trials
- large observational studies with transparent methods
- drug/device labels, safety communications, and adverse event warnings

Treat as lower confidence:

- single small studies
- preprints
- clinic marketing pages
- anecdotal reports
- influencer or forum claims

## Clinical Evidence Checks

For important claims, inspect:

- population, intervention, comparator, outcome, and follow-up period
- sample size, randomization, blinding, endpoints, and statistical significance
- absolute risk, relative risk, adverse events, and contraindications
- conflicts of interest and funding
- whether findings apply to the user's geography, age group, or context

## Output Checks

- Separate general research from personal medical advice.
- Include source type and evidence level.
- Include safety caveats when appropriate.
- If evidence conflicts, state the conflict and what higher-quality sources say.
